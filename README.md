## What is Ketamine HCl?
Ketamine HCl, or hydrochloride, is a dissociative anesthetic medication primarily used in medical settings. Discovered in the 1960s, it has gained renewed attention in 2026 for its potential in treating conditions like depression, chronic pain, and PTSD. Unlike traditional anesthetics, ketamine works by blocking NMDA receptors in the brain, providing rapid effects with a lower risk of respiratory depression.

In therapeutic doses, it's administered via injection, nasal spray, or infusion under medical supervision. However, recreational or unregulated use can lead to side effects such as hallucinations, increased heart rate, and dependency. As of 2026, the FDA has approved certain ketamine-derived treatments, like esketamine (Spravato), for treatment-resistant depression.
